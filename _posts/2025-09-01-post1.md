There is a moment early in university when a professor says something that stays with you long after the lecture ends. For me, that moment came in the first semester at UET Lahore, Faisalabad Campus, when **Dr. Bilal Ahmad** suggested that his students document their university journey online. Not for grades. Not for show. For themselves.

He called it leaving a digital footprint. The idea was simple: you are already going through experiences, making mistakes, learning things, and forming opinions about your field. Why let all of that disappear? A blog forces you to slow down, reflect, and turn scattered notes and memories into something structured — something useful to your future self and to whoever comes after you.

I am Daniyal Nawaz, a Computer Engineering student at UET Lahore, Faisalabad Campus. When Dr. Bilal Ahmad made that suggestion in class, I decided to take it seriously. This blog is the result.

Dr. Bilal Ahmad teaches Programming Fundamentals and Database Management Systems in our department. Beyond the classroom, he is an active researcher in Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning — with a particular focus on medical datasets, which he values for the precision and real-world significance with which they are collected. That combination of teaching and research gives him a perspective on learning that most professors do not have. He does not just teach concepts — he shows you where those concepts lead. You can follow his academic work on [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com.au/citations?user=8nZ0jVkAAAAJ&hl=en).

Writing about what you are learning deepens your understanding of it. When I tried to explain in words how a Machine Learning model works, or what normalisation in databases actually achieves, I realised how much of my understanding was still fuzzy. Writing forced me to close those gaps. That is a benefit no exam can replicate.

There is also a professional dimension to this. We live in an age where your online presence matters. Future employers and collaborators will search your name. A well-maintained blog showing two years of genuine learning in Computer Engineering — real projects, real reflections, real growth — is a different conversation from an empty search result.

This blog covers two full semesters. In the first, I went from scoring 2/10 in my opening programming quiz to presenting a Machine Learning model at a department exhibition and finishing with a B+ in Programming Fundamentals. In the second, I worked on database design, normalisation, ER diagrams, and an Earthquake Prediction System built using ML. Both semesters had hardships, breakthroughs, and lessons I intend to carry forward.

Every post on this blog is written in my own voice, from my own experience. I hope it is useful to someone who is where I was — nervous, uncertain, and trying to figure out whether they belong in this field. You do. Keep going.
