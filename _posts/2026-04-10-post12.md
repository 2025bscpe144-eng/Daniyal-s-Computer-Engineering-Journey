Two semesters of Computer Engineering at UET Lahore, Faisalabad Campus are behind me now. Two GPAs recorded. Two ML projects built and presented. Two sets of hardships survived and, in time, understood. Looking back across both semesters from this point, the shape of the journey is clearer than it ever was while I was living through it.

Semester one gave me the foundations — **Programming Fundamentals** under **Dr. Bilal Ahmad**, a painful but necessary set of early failures, and a project that proved recovery is always possible. The Car Price Prediction model, trained on a USA car dataset, achieved 89% accuracy and scored 8/10 at the final presentation — highest in the class. The semester GPA was 3.08. The programming grade was B+. From 2/10 to B+ is a story I intend to remember.

Semester two built on those foundations in every direction. **Database Management Systems**, again under Dr. Bilal Ahmad, introduced me to a different way of thinking about data — not as something that just exists, but as something that must be designed, structured, and maintained carefully before it can be trusted. Normalisation, ER diagrams, relational schema design — these are tools I now use instinctively when approaching any data problem. The Earthquake Prediction System brought everything together: Kaggle dataset, cleaned and normalised, database designed from an ER diagram, ML model trained and evaluated, results interpreted against real seismological knowledge.

Alongside those two core courses, OOP gave me experience building a University Admission Management System and a GUI-based game — applications of object-oriented principles to real software problems. DLD gave me a 4-bit hardware adder-subtractor and a deeper appreciation for what happens at the gate level beneath all the code we write. Communication Skills, the Annual Fiesta (where I served as **captain of my department team**), and the Welcome Party rounded out a semester that was richer and more demanding than the first.

**Dr. Bilal Ahmad** has been the most consistent presence across both semesters. His teaching of PF and DBMS, his research focus on AI, ML, and DL with medical and high-precision datasets, and his insistence that students leave a digital footprint — this blog exists because of that last point. I am grateful for it. Writing these posts has made me understand my own experience more clearly than I would have otherwise. You can follow his work on [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com.au/citations?user=8nZ0jVkAAAAJ&hl=en) and connect with him on [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/drbilalphd/).

My target CGPA going forward is 3.3 or above, In sha Allah. That requires consistency, earlier preparation, and the study habits I have now built through two semesters of learning what not to do. The remaining semesters will bring more complexity, more projects, and more opportunities to grow. I intend to document all of it here.

To anyone reading this who is at the beginning of their CE journey — the nervousness you feel in Week 1 is normal. The 2/10 quiz result that might come in Week 5 is survivable. The recovery that follows is possible. And the documentation of the whole journey, however imperfect, is worth doing.

Keep going.
