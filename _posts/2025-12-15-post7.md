Semester one is done. GPA: **3.08**, Alhamdulillah. B+ in Programming Fundamentals — the subject that gave me 2/10 in my opening quiz. The lab manuals are bound and submitted. The exhibition is over. The cake has been cut. And sitting here writing this, I feel a clarity about those fourteen weeks that was impossible to feel while living through them.

Let me be honest about the 3.08. It is not where I wanted to finish. There is a version of this semester — one where I prepared earlier, engaged with programming labs more actively from Week 2, and approached midterms with understanding rather than rote memorisation — that produces a higher number. I know that because I know exactly where the marks went. The midterm preparation strategy (or lack of one), the slow start to genuinely engaging with Python, the 11/40 that reflected cramming rather than understanding — all of those had consequences that carried into the final GPA.

That is accountability, not regret. The 3.08 is an accurate record of how I spent the semester, including the weeks I wasted and the weeks I used well.

What the GPA does not capture is the B+ in Programming Fundamentals — earned after a 2/10 quiz and an 11/40 midterm, through a project that required genuine understanding, a presentation scored highest in class, and a department exhibition appearance. That B+ means more to me than any grade I received through straightforward performance. It represents a recovery, and recoveries reveal more about a person than smooth trajectories do.

**Dr. Bilal Ahmad** — whose expertise in AI, ML, and Deep Learning, and whose research you can explore on [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com.au/citations?user=8nZ0jVkAAAAJ&hl=en) — designed the semester in a way that allowed for that recovery. The project was the mechanism. It gave students a chance to demonstrate understanding that exams had not captured.

Semester two is ahead. New courses: **Database Management Systems** (again with Dr. Bilal Ahmad) and Object Oriented Programming, among others. New project: an Earthquake Prediction System. New goals: a CGPA of 3.3 or above, In sha Allah — earned through better preparation habits, earlier starts, and the understanding that consistent effort across fourteen weeks always beats a last-minute sprint.

The lessons of semester one are specific and practical. Start assignments earlier. Engage with labs actively. Ask more questions out loud. And document the journey — because writing about what you are learning deepens your understanding of it in ways that nothing else does.

See you in semester two.
